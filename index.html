Felipe. Yo soy Felipe. Felipe. I am Felipe.

Felipe. Soy Felipe. Felipe. I am Felipe.

Felipe, soy Felipe. Felipe, I am Felipe.

Día 1. Parte 3

Yo tengo treinta años. I am thirty years old.

¿Tengo yo diez años? Am I ten years old?

No, yo no tengo diez años. Tengo treinta años. No, I am not ten years old. I am thirty years old.

¿Tengo yo treinta años? Am I thirty years old?

Sí. Yo tengo treinta años. Yes. I am thirty years old.

¿Quién tiene treinta años? Who is thirty years old?

Yo. Yo tengo treinta años. Me. I am thirty years old.

¿Cuántos años tengo? How old am I?

Treinta. Yo tengo treinta años. Tengo treinta años. Thirty. I am thirty years old. I am thirty years old.

¿Quién tiene treinta años? Who is thirty years old?

Yo. Yo, Felipe, tengo treinta años. Me. I, Felipe, am thirty years old.

How to Say “I Am” and “I Have” in Spanish

To say “I am Felipe,” you literally say “I am Felipe” – “Yo soy Felipe.”

Verbs in Spanish change according to the person. For example, the verb “to be” – “ser”: “Yo (I) soy”; “Él (he) es Felipe.”

The verb “to have” is “tener.” “Yo tengo” – I have; “Él tiene” – he has.

¡No! Yo no soy una mujer. Soy un hombre. No! I am not a woman. I am a man.

¿Soy yo un hombre o una mujer? Am I a man or a woman?

¡Un hombre! Yo soy un hombre. Soy un hombre. A man! I am a man. I am a man.

Yo. Yo soy un hombre. Me. I am a man.

Oscar’s Comment

New course, new method... Don’t worry, you’ll get used to it soon. As you can see, your task is simple – listen several times. This is the way we learned our native language as children: we heard the same expressions from our parents again and again. Repetition is necessary to form patterns in our brain. You’ll be surprised how quickly you’ll start getting used to new words and structures. Just keep doing this simple task!

How to Ask Questions and Make Negative Sentences in Spanish

To ask a question, you need to swap the subject and the verb: “Yo quiero.” → “¿Quiero yo?”

But this is not a strict rule, so (just like in Russian) you can indicate a question with your intonation: “Yo quiero?” → “¿Yo quiero?”

By the way, the punctuation marks at the beginning and end of the sentence are a feature of Spanish punctuation.

To form a negative sentence, just like in Russian, you simply place the particle "no" between the subject and the verb: “Yo no quiero” → “I don't want to.”
